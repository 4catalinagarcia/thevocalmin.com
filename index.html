The Book

What Happens When the World Doesn’t Want You to Speak

The Vocal Minority is the raw, unflinching memoir of Jeff Kohler — a man who spent decades navigating institutions, communities, and relationships that had a vested interest in his silence.

From the halls of power to the margins of society, Jeff’s story is one of hard-won identity, relentless truth-telling, and the cost that comes with refusing to disappear.

This is not a comfortable book. It was not a comfortable life. But it is an honest one.

The Author

Jeff Kohler

Jeff Kohler is a writer, advocate, and lifelong truth-teller. Over the course of his life he has moved through worlds that rarely overlap — finding himself at the intersection of politics, community, and personal identity.

The Vocal Minority is his first book — a memoir years in the making and impossible to contain any longer.

Jeff writes about the experiences that shaped him, the systems that tried to define him, and the voice he refused to surrender.

A Glimpse Inside the Pages

From the Opening

There is a particular kind of silence that is manufactured. Not the silence of an empty room, or the silence between words when someone is thinking. This silence is built — brick by brick, policy by policy, dismissal by dismissal — by people who need you to disappear.

I spent the better part of my life learning to recognize it. And then I spent the rest of it refusing to honor it.

This is not a book about being a victim. It is a book about survival in the truest sense of the word: not merely enduring, but insisting on being seen.
